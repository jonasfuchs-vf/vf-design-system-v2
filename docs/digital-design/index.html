Digital Design

#Establish a common web and mobile web experience

The objective of Web Simplification 2.0 is to enable Vodafone to establish a common web and mobile web experience driving brand consistency across all local markets. The programme will also be responsible for helping to drive improvement to market commercial and performance KPI’s including: TNPS, First contact resolution (FCR) and page download speed.

For the creation of a concept and visual design this means the following:

Be CONTEXTUAL

Ensure that users are contextually aware of where they are within their journey.

Be SIMPLE

Reduce the user’s cognitive workload whenever possible. Be consistent and clear, and establish a strong visual hierarchy.

Be FINDABLE

Establish a strong information scent. Provide way-finding signs to guide users to where they want to go.

Be RELEVANT

Surface the right information at the right moment. Provide guidance and suggestions in a proactive way.

Be PERSONAL

Establish a strong information scent. Provide way-finding signs to guide users to where they want to go.

Design in Web Simplification 2.0

This guideline describes the user interface of Web Simplification 2.0 for our lokal market (Germany) and defines the Design elements specifying the single Design Tokens as itself that will enable the UX designers in the different teams to create a consistent layout.

If you create a WS2 style design for our website, there are a few simple basic rules to follow:

Responsive Design

To make our content available without limitations, our pages are responsive with a fluid grid. We have 4 breakpoints which influence the layout and the conception. During the conception we consider special viewports for the minimum and maximum characteristics of a layout and try to find a good compromise.

Colors

As the Vodafone red is the main color use a combination of color, shapes and text to create an indication for an interaction, selection or highlight. Keep the balance of the amount of red elements on a page and be carful with the context. Important elements should be clearly recognisable and stand out. Use a good color contrasts and a readable font size.

Opacity

We use three levels of opacity from low to strong for different purposes. The objective is always to create distinction and contrast and make certain elements stand out. Transparency can be used to darken to bring another element into the foreground, but also as visual feedback for an interaction such as a hover.

Typography

Typography is an important part of a layout and especially digitally there are some rules to be observed. A sufficient contrast and a sufficiently large font is mandatory. To create a clear hierarchy and page structure we have a clearly definition of headlines (1-5) and text for content (in 16px) considering the responsive behaviour.

Spacings

We need spacing so that design can have an effect and common elements can be visually divided into groups. Clickable elements should be designed large and have enough space. It is especially important to create a good usability for mobile applications. Our spacing is based on the belt of our grid. Derived from this we have different values which may be used.

Shadows

A shadow is created by light that is blocked by an object. The size of a shadow indicates how high the object is positioned above the base. Higher objects have larger shadows than objects that are lower. Elements with large shadows can help to separate the single layers of a design. Smaller shadows show that elements are more important and clickable.

Borders

Our borders can serve various purposes. On the one hand as decoration like clustering and on the other hand as an indicator for interaction. The contour is determined by its width and the radius of the corners, which can also be rounded. We have defined certain values for both.

Iconography

Clean and graphically simple, our distinct 'continuous line' icons are used to help customers navigate quickly and easily within our digital applications. Inspiration for the rounded corners and ends, as well as, the angles of the icons have been taken from the Vodafone brand. The icons are designed as metaphors to make them easy to understand.

Layout Composing

To create a design that reflects the way of Vodafone, create simple, consistent and logical layouts. Keep the content short, clear and simple and don't tire users with lots of typing and scrolling. Break up content with sub-headings, diagrams, images and videos.
